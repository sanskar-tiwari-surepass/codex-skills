# Wikipedia Signs of AI Writing Notes

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Signs_of_AI_writing
Accessed: 2026-03-25

Adapt this note for general content writing, not Wikipedia editing. Use it as a quality filter, not as a detector or accusation tool.

## Core idea

LLM prose often gets less specific while sounding more important. It smooths sharp details into generic language, then compensates with confidence, uplift, and summary rhetoric.

## Content patterns to avoid

- Inflated significance, legacy, or "broader trend" framing that makes ordinary details sound historic.
- Promotional puffery, brochure language, and generic praise.
- Superficial analysis that gestures at impact without naming mechanism or stakes.
- Vague attributions such as "experts say", "many believe", or "widely regarded" with no named source.
- Boilerplate endings that default to challenges, opportunities, or future prospects.

## Language patterns to avoid

- Dense clusters of stock AI vocabulary: pivotal, vibrant, notable, crucial, underscores, evolving landscape, rich tapestry, multifaceted, realm, delve, showcase.
- Chronic avoidance of plain "is" and "are" sentences in favor of inflated phrasing.
- Contrast templates such as "not just X, but Y" and "not X, but Y".
- Repeated rule-of-three constructions.
- Decorative synonym swaps that blur meaning instead of improving rhythm.

## Style and formatting patterns to avoid

- Title case for headings that do not need it.
- Excess boldface.
- Outline residue that reads like headings pasted from a generated plan.
- Emoji or heavy em-dash use when the target voice does not call for it.
- Chatbot-facing phrasing inside the deliverable rather than in the surrounding conversation.

## Fast rewrite moves

- Replace abstract importance claims with one observable consequence.
- Replace general praise with one specific attribute, action, or example.
- Replace vague social proof with a named person, publication, customer, or source.
- Delete ceremonial conclusions unless they add a new idea.
- Compress transitions that explain too much about the structure of the piece.

## Final self-check

- Could this paragraph fit ten unrelated topics with only the nouns swapped out?
- Did I say why something matters, or did I merely announce that it matters?
- Does the ending earn its place?
- Would a good editor cut any adjective here as empty emphasis?
